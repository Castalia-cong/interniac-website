It Shouldn't be That Hard to Get Work Experience

Given how competitive college admissions are and how often the average American college student changes majors due to career confusion, getting a high school internship is more valuable than ever before. However, pre-college work experience is difficult to come by and oftentimes opportunities are given to students who have strong networks (either through a parent or their school). Today, there is no platform through which high school students all over the country can find an internship on their own. We aim to build a platform that supports high schoolers attending public schools and empowers businesses in hiring bright students. ConnectX fills the gap in our education system by simplifying the process for employers to start internship programs and source candidates who are eager to gain valuable out-of-the-classroom work experience.

Seven Habits of Highly Effective Teens

As we aspire to be good interns, let’s remember to be great people.

  • Habit One: Be Proactive
  • Habit Two: Begin with the End in Mind
  • Habit Three: Put First Things First
  • Habit Four: Think Win-Win
  • Habit Five: Seek First to Understand, Then to be Understood
  • Habit Six: Synergize
  • Habit Seven: Sharpen the Saw
The choice is yours.

Be proactive, not reactive. Proactive people make choices based on their values, think before they act. They have an internal locus of control and recognize what falls outside of their control.
Control your own destiny or someone else will.

Develop a clear picture of where you want to tale your life. Set goals, understand your values. Not necessarily a career or college decision, but specifically the direction you’d like in life. Take each step in the right direction.
Will and won’t power.

Learn to manage and prioritize your time so that your first things first come first and not last.
Life is an all-you-can-eat buffet.

This is the foundation for getting along well with people. This begins with the belief that we are all equal, no one is inferior or superior and no one needs to be. Plenty of success to go around, it’s about “both”.
You have two ears and one mouth.

Listen with your eyes, heart, and ears. Make sure you understand a problem before attempting to solve it.
The “high” way.

Work with more people to create a solution better than you can alone. It’s not your way or their way, it’s the “higher” way.
It’s “Me Time”.

Care for your body, mind, heart, and soul. Stay balanced and continue sharpening all aspects of your identity. It’s okay not to know who you are, but keep yourself in the phase of discovery with a healthy mindset.
