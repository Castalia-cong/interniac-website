REAL ESTATE AGENT

What does a Real Estate Agent do?

Real estate agents arrange the selling/renting of property. They can help put buyers and sellers together and negotiate for their clients. Every time they successfully sell off a property, they are compensated with a percentage of the property’s purchase price. In this way, a real estate agent’s income is not entirely secure; their income is based on their ability to close a deal. Many states require agents to work with a real estate broker, a more experienced person or a brokerage firm. A real estate agent must have excellent communication skills as they will be talking with clients, other real estate agents, landlords, and more. They also need to be organized to arrange meetings between clients and sellers/renters. Real estate agents research properties and deal with real estate documents.

How do you become a Real Estate Agent?
  1. Look into your state’s licensing requirements.
    • Age restrictions and other requirements
  2. Take a pre-licensing course.
    • These can be taken at a variety of real estate schools, universities, or home study programs.
  3. Take and pass the real estate salesperson examination.
    • Applying for the test can be a long, tedious process that may even require you to submit fingerprints and pass a background check. Requirements for application, again, vary from state to state and may require a fee.
    • To pass the exam, it is highly encouraged to apply for an exam prep course.
  4. Find a real estate broker.
    • After you pass your real estate salesperson examination and find a real estate broker, you can start practicing real estate under the sponsorship of a broker.
